Active manuscript line

Attention, Autonomy, and Justice

This research line asks when attention becomes a matter of justice rather than only a psychological, market, design, or media-ethics concern. It examines how attention capture can affect autonomy, fair opportunity, democratic participation, and equal standing among persons.

Status

This is an active manuscript-development line. The internal manuscript family currently preserved for future work is titled The Normative Structure of Attention: A Multi-Faceted Account of Attentional Justice.

This public page is an orientation to the research agenda. It is not a public manuscript, draft preprint, or citable version of the paper.

Core Questions

  • When does attention capture become a justice-relevant wrong?
  • How do attention environments shape autonomy and reflective agency?
  • Can patterned visibility, salience, and distraction become part of the opportunity structure?
  • How should democratic theory understand fragmented or platform-mediated public attention?
  • What distinguishes ordinary influence from institutionally structured manipulation or capture?

Orientation

  • Attention is treated as a condition of agency and social standing, not merely as a private mental resource.
  • The project connects autonomy, manipulation, fair opportunity, democratic attention, and platform governance.
  • The current task is to identify the threshold at which attention-shaping structures become matters of justice.
