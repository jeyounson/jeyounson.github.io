Bio

My research focuses on moral and legal philosophy, with a particular emphasis on social cooperation, legal institutions, and distributive justice. I explore philosophical critiques of legal systems, normative authority, and justice through the lens of contemporary German social philosophy and European philosophical debates. My recent work includes studies on human dignity, legal expressivism, and institutional aspects of social philosophy, aiming to creatively reinterpret current debates in these areas.

I am a researcher based in South Korea, specializing in legal philosophy. I earned my Ph.D. in Law with a focus on legal philosophy and the concept of Human Dignity. Prior to this, I completed my undergraduate studies in law and master’s degree in western philosophy, emphasizing Kant's ethical thoughts. I have actively contributed to academic discussions, publishing on topics such as normative reasons, moral realism, and the institutional dimensions of rights and duties. My professional experience includes teaching and designing syllabi for courses on social philosophy and legal theory.

Additionally, I currently teach undergraduate students in argumentative writing, focusing on developing their skills in crafting clear, persuasive arguments. I have also specialized in methodologies for teaching argumentation, ensuring students gain practical and theoretical proficiency in this critical area. You can find my lecture videos on writing here.
